# Privacy Policy

**Last updated:** [DATE]

**Ohana Handshake** (“**we**,” “**us**,” or “**our**”) operates a decentralized application (dapp) for vouching on public blockchains. This Privacy Policy explains how we approach data in the context of our Service. **A core principle: data recorded on public blockchains is on-chain and public by design.** We do not operate a traditional “backend” that stores your personal data; the primary data related to vouches and profiles lives on public blockchains and is visible to anyone.

---

## 1. Summary

- **On-chain data is public.** Vouches, acceptances, denials, and related contract state are written to public blockchains and can be read by anyone. We do not control or delete this data.
- **We do not collect or store your personal data on our servers.** We do not run servers that store your name, email, or other identifying information you provide elsewhere.
- **Local browser storage.** The dapp may use your browser’s local storage (e.g., theme preference, local “hidden vouches” settings) only on your device. This is not sent to our servers.
- **We do not sell your data.** We have no business model based on selling user data.

---

## 2. Data That Is Public by Design (On-Chain)

When you use the Service to vouch, accept, deny, hide, or remove vouches, or when you store a Handshake reference on your Universal Profile (e.g., via LSP2), the following are recorded on public blockchains and are **permanent and publicly visible**:

- Wallet addresses involved in transactions (voucher, recipient).
- Vouch status, category, and related contract state (e.g., hidden flag).
- Transaction hashes, block numbers, and timestamps.
- Any data you choose to write to your Universal Profile (e.g., LSP4, LSP26) or to contract storage.

**We do not control blockchain networks or smart contracts.** Once data is on-chain, it can be copied, indexed, and used by third parties (explorers, indexers, other dapps). We cannot delete or make on-chain data private.

---

## 3. Data We Do Not Collect on Our Servers

We do not operate backend servers that collect or store:

- Your name, email, phone number, or physical address.
- Your wallet private keys, recovery phrases, or passwords.
- Your on-chain transaction history (we read it from public chains like any user can).
- Any “account” or “profile” data beyond what is already public on-chain or in your wallet.

If our Service uses analytics or error-reporting tools (e.g., client-side only), we would describe that below. Otherwise, we do not track you across the web for advertising or sell your data.

---

## 4. Data Stored Only in Your Browser (Local Storage)

The dapp may use your browser’s **local storage** (or similar mechanisms) solely on your device, for example:

- **Theme preference** (e.g., dark, light, cyberpunk) so your choice persists across visits.
- **Local “hidden vouches” list** (if used before or in addition to on-chain hiding), so the interface can reflect your preferences on that device.

This data:

- Stays on your device and is not sent to our servers (we do not have servers that receive it).
- Can be cleared by you (e.g., clearing site data or local storage for our domain).
- Is not used by us for tracking or advertising.

We do not have access to or control over this data once it is on your device.

---

## 5. Wallet Connection and Third-Party Wallets

When you connect a wallet (e.g., MetaMask, Universal Profile extension):

- **We do not receive your private keys or recovery phrases.** Connection and signing happen in your wallet; we only receive the public address(es) and chain state that the wallet exposes to the dapp.
- **Your wallet provider** (e.g., MetaMask, LUKSO) has its own privacy practices. We encourage you to read their privacy policies.
- **Public address.** Your wallet’s public address is visible to the dapp and to anyone who can see the screen or the network requests from your browser. On-chain activity tied to that address is already public on the blockchain.

---

## 6. Third-Party Services and Data They May See

The Service may interact with:

- **RPC providers / node providers:** When the dapp reads or sends transactions, those requests (including your IP and the data in the request) may be visible to the RPC provider. We do not control their logging or privacy policies.
- **Wallet extensions and their infrastructure:** Subject to each provider’s privacy policy.
- **Universal Profile / LSP data:** Profile data (e.g., LSP4, LSP26) is stored on-chain or on decentralized storage (e.g., IPFS); the dapp may fetch it via public gateways. Those gateways may log requests (e.g., IP, resource requested).
- **Block explorers and indexers:** Any party can read public blockchain data and may aggregate or publish it.

We are not responsible for the data practices of these third parties. We recommend reviewing their privacy policies if you are concerned about what they collect or retain.

---

## 7. Cookies and Similar Technologies

If we use cookies or similar technologies (e.g., for session or preference storage), we use them only to operate the dapp (e.g., remembering theme or local preferences) and not for cross-site tracking or advertising. You can control or delete cookies via your browser settings.

---

## 8. Your Rights and Choices

- **On-chain data:** You can choose what you submit on-chain (e.g., whether to vouch, accept, or store data on your Universal Profile). Once submitted, it is public and we cannot delete it.
- **Local storage:** You can clear local storage (or site data) for our dapp in your browser to remove theme or other local preferences.
- **Wallet:** You can disconnect your wallet at any time from the dapp. We do not retain wallet data on our side beyond what is in your browser session.
- **Jurisdiction-specific rights:** If you are in the European Economic Area, United Kingdom, or another jurisdiction with data protection laws, you may have rights such as access, rectification, or erasure. Because we do not collect or store your personal data on our servers, there is little for us to “process” on your behalf; on-chain data is public and not under our control. For any request, you can contact us using the details below.

---

## 9. Data Security

We do not store your personal data on our servers. You are responsible for securing your own device, browser, and wallet (e.g., private keys, recovery phrase). We recommend using security best practices (e.g., hardware wallet, strong device security) when transacting on public blockchains.
